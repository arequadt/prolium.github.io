Pipeline

Prolium’s first asset is PRO-203, a CD20xCD3 bispecific antibody. It works by binding both CD20, a protein that is broadly expressed on the surface of B-cells, and CD3, which is part of the T-cell receptor complex on T cells and is crucial for T-cell activation. Physically linking T-cells to B-cells causes T-cells to release cytotoxic molecules to kill the B-cell, even though the T-cell might not otherwise ordinarily recognize the B-cell as a target.

Originally developed by InnoCare and Keymed, PRO-203 was first studied in relapsed / refractory Non-Hodgkin’s lymphoma (NHL). Prolium plans to develop PRO-203, administered subcutaneously, for severe autoimmune diseases that are driven predominantly by aberrant B-cells.

Prolium is actively evaluating additional therapies that have the potential to treat severe autoimmune disease.

The Potential of T-Cell Engagers as a Therapeutic Approach

T-cell engagers (TCEs) are engineered, bispecific antibodies that redirect T-cells to eliminate cells expressing a specific antigen. While initially pioneered in oncology to destroy tumor cells, TCEs are now being explored for autoimmune diseases, where the goal is to selectively deplete pathogenic immune cells.

TCEs are typically bispecific antibodies or antibody fragments designed with:

  • One arm binding CD3: a component of the T-cell receptor (TCR) complex on all T-cells.
  • One arm binding a disease-relevant target antigen, such as CD20 or CD19, expressed on B-cells.

When both arms engage their targets simultaneously, the TCE forms an immune synapse between the T-cell and the target cell, triggering T-cell activation, release of cytotoxic granules, and targeted killing of the bound cell — independent of MHC or traditional antigen recognition.

T-cell engagers offer a transformative approach for autoimmunity by enabling precision depletion of disease-driving cells. By targeting well-validated antigens, TCEs may combine the potency of cellular immunotherapy with the precision of biologics — ushering in a new class of therapies for patients unresponsive to conventional immunosuppressants.
